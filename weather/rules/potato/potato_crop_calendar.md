# Potato Crop Calendar — Kenya

**For agronomist review**
Version 1.0.0 | 3 April 2026

This calendar describes the month-by-month management activities, growth stages, and pest/disease risk levels for potato production in Kenya's highlands (1,500–3,000m ASL). Two crops per year, aligned to bimodal rainfall.

**Sources referenced throughout:**
- **NPCK** — Climate Resilient Potato Production Handbook, NPCK & CRAFT
- **TRAINING** — Final Potato Training Manual

**Important notes:**
- Timing shifts ±2 weeks depending on altitude and rainfall onset
- Crop duration: 120–150 days depending on variety
- Two crops per year: Long Rains (main) and Short Rains (secondary)

---

## Kenya Seasons

| Season | Months | Potato Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Long Rains | March – May | Main planting season. Plant Mar–Apr, grow through wet season. |
| Long Dry | June – August | Long rains crop: harvest. Land drying, curing, storage. |
| Short Dry | September, Jan – Feb | Land preparation. Seed procurement. Short rains crop: harvest (Jan–Feb). |
| Short Rains | October – December | Second planting season. Plant Oct–Nov, grow through wet season. |

## Monthly Calendar

### January — Short Dry

**Long rains crop:** Storage | **Short rains crop:** Harvest

| Activity | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Harvest | **Critical** | Short rains crop ready. Harvest only in dry weather with cloud cover. Thumb test for skin set. |
| Pest monitoring | **High** | PTM risk HIGH in warm dry conditions. Check ridges. Inspect stored tubers. |
| Planting prep | Low | Prepare land for long rains. Plough during dry season. Soil samples (dry soil only). |

| Threat | Risk |
|---|---|
| Late blight | Low |
| Aphid | Moderate |
| PTM | **High** |

---

### February — Short Dry

**Long rains crop:** Land prep | **Short rains crop:** Curing

| Activity | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Harvest | **High** | Complete short rains harvest. Cure at 18–20°C, 85–90% RH, 10–14 days. |
| Planting prep | **High** | Plough 15–25cm. Harrow 2 weeks later. Apply lime if pH <6.0. Apply manure. |

| Threat | Risk |
|---|---|
| Late blight | Low |
| PTM | Moderate |

---

### March — Long Rains Begin

**Long rains crop:** Planting → emergence | **Short rains crop:** Storage

| Activity | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Planting | **Critical** | **PLANT NOW** at rain onset. Well-sprouted seed (3+ sprouts). 30cm spacing, 15cm depth. DAP at planting. |
| Spraying | **High** | Begin Mancozeb from emergence. Every 7 days. First insecticide at 10 days after germination. |
| Pest monitoring | **High** | Set up 6 yellow water traps/acre. Scout for cutworms at emergence (early morning). |

| Threat | Risk |
|---|---|
| Late blight | Rising |
| Bacterial wilt | Moderate |
| Cutworm | **High** |

---

### April — Long Rains (Peak)

**Long rains crop:** Vegetative

| Activity | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Spraying | **Critical** | Mancozeb every 7 days — peak blight risk. Alternate with Metalaxyl. Do NOT skip. |
| Fertilizer | **High** | First hilling + weeding at 20cm height. CAN top-dress if N deficiency visible. |
| Pest monitoring | **High** | Check aphid traps weekly. Spray if detected. Scout for cutworm damage. |

| Threat | Risk |
|---|---|
| Late blight | **Very High** |
| Bacterial wilt | **High** |
| Blackleg | **High** |
| Aphid | Moderate |

---

### May — Long Rains (Ending)

**Long rains crop:** Tuberization → bulking

| Activity | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Spraying | **Critical** | Continue fungicide every 7 days. Blight risk remains very high. |
| Irrigation | Medium | Soil moisture CRITICAL at tuber set. Irrigate if dry spell. Dry soil = common scab. |
| Fertilizer | Medium | Second hilling 2–3 weeks after first. Top-dress N before bulking if deficient. |
| Pest monitoring | **High** | Aphid risk rising as rains ease. PTM check — re-earth up if tubers exposed. |

| Threat | Risk |
|---|---|
| Late blight | **Very High** |
| Common scab | Moderate |
| Aphid | Rising |
| PTM | Moderate |

## Growth Stage Definitions

| Stage | Duration | Description | Critical Conditions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Land preparation | 3 weeks before planting | Plough 15–25cm, harrow, lime, manure | Dry soil for tillage |
| Planting | 1 day | Well-sprouted seed, 30cm spacing, 15cm depth, DAP | Soil ≥8°C, not waterlogged |
| Emergence | ~14 days | Shoots emerge. First weeding begins. | Cutworm scouting critical |
| Vegetative | 2–8 weeks after planting | Rapid growth. Hilling. Fungicide every 7 days. | Late blight peak risk |
| Tuberization | 6–8 weeks after planting | Tuber initiation. | Soil 15–24°C. Moisture critical (scab). Declines >21°C. |
| Bulking | 8–14 weeks | Tubers enlarge. Highest N demand. | PTM risk if tubers exposed |
| Maturation | 14–20 weeks | Haulms yellow. Dehaulm. Skin hardening. | ≥50% haulms yellow = dehaulm |
| Harvest | 16–22 weeks | Dry weather, cloud cover. Thumb test. | No wet harvest. Shade within 3h. |
| Curing | 10–14 days post-harvest | 18–20°C, 85–90% RH. Wound healing. | Ventilated shed |
| Storage (ware) | Months | 4–7°C, 95% RH, dark | PTM monitoring |
| Storage (seed) | Until planting | Diffused light store (DLS), cool, 2–3 layers | Aphid monitoring |

## Variety Information

### Blight-Resistant Varieties
Kenya Faulu, Kenya Karibu, Chaguo, Kenya Mavuno, Kenya Sifa, Tigoni, Asante

### Maturity Classes
| Class | Days to Maturity | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Early | 90–110 days | Shortened seasons, late planting, drought escape |
| Medium | 110–130 days | Standard seasons (Shangi, Tigoni) |
| Late | 130–150 days | Full-length seasons with reliable rainfall |

### Processing Suitability
| Use | Suitable Varieties |
|---|---|
| Table (fresh) | Shangi, Kenya Karibu, Kenya Mavuno, Tigoni, Unica |
| Chips (French fries) | Wanjiku, Shangi, Sherekea, Unica, Manitou |
| Crisps | Wanjiku, Sherekea, Konjo, Lenana, Dutch Robijn |

## Spray Schedule

### Fungicide
| Product | Start | Interval | Stop | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mancozeb | At crop emergence | Every 7 days | At dehaulming | 6 knapsacks/acre |
| Metalaxyl | Alternate with Mancozeb | Every 7 days | At dehaulming | Per label |

**Total sprays per season:** Up to 10

### Insecticide
| Product | Rate | Start | Targets |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alphacypermethrin | 30–40ml per 20L water | 10 days after germination | Aphids, cutworms, PTM |
| Dimethoate | 30–40ml per 20L water | 10 days after germination | Aphids, cutworms, PTM |

## How This Calendar Is Used by the Advisory System

This calendar is not sent to the LLM. It runs in the Python rule engine:

1. System determines which crop the farmer is growing and which planting season they're in
2. Current month → growth stage lookup for that season's crop
3. Weather rules filtered by growth stage (e.g., no harvest rules during vegetative stage)
4. Calendar management tasks (critical/high) converted into weather-gated advice
5. Only triggered advice (5–10 items) goes into the LLM prompt for translation to farmer language
