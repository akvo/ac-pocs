# Potato Weather Advisory Rules — Kenya

**For agronomist review**
Version 1.0.0 | 3 April 2026 | 30 rules total

This document describes the weather-triggered advisory rules used by our system to generate recommendations for potato farmers in Kenya's highlands (1,500–3,000m ASL). Each rule monitors specific weather conditions and, when triggered, produces actionable advice.

**Sources referenced throughout:**
- **NPCK** — Climate Resilient Potato Production Handbook, NPCK & CRAFT (136 pages)
- **TRAINING** — Final Potato Training Manual (82 pages)

**Priority levels used:**
| Priority | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Critical | Immediate action required — significant crop loss possible |
| High | Action needed within 24-48 hours |
| Medium | Monitor and act within the week |
| Opportunity | Favourable window — take advantage now |
| Informational | No action needed — background knowledge |

## 1. Late Blight

**LB-001 — Infection Trigger**
- **Triggers when:** Temperature ≥10°C for 2+ consecutive days AND humidity ≥90%
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking, maturation
- **Why it matters:** Phytophthora infestans infection triggered. Late blight can destroy entire crop within days if untreated.
- **Priority:** Critical
- **What to do:**
  1. Apply Mancozeb fungicide immediately if not sprayed in last 7 days
  2. For susceptible varieties: spray every 7 days from emergence — do NOT wait for symptoms
  3. For resistant varieties (Kenya Faulu, Karibu, Chaguo, Mavuno, Sifa, Tigoni, Asante): spray when symptoms first appear
  4. Alternate Mancozeb with Metalaxyl to prevent resistance
- **Sources:** NPCK p.88-89

**LB-002 — Accelerated Spread**
- **Triggers when:** Temperature ≥15°C AND humidity ≥85%
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Warm + humid accelerates blight spread. Infection can move from first spots to total crop loss rapidly.
- **Priority:** Critical
- **What to do:**
  1. Reduce spray interval to every 7 days or less
  2. Scout daily for grey spots on lower leaf tips and edges
  3. Do NOT use sprinkler irrigation — wet canopy promotes blight
  4. Switch to drip or furrow irrigation
- **Sources:** NPCK p.88

**LB-003 — Spray Window**
- **Triggers when:** Rain probability in next 6 hours ≤20% AND wind <15 km/h
- **Applies to:** All stages from emergence
- **This is an opportunity window.**
- **Priority:** Opportunity
- **What to do:**
  1. Good window to apply Mancozeb — calm and dry for good coverage
  2. Spray early morning or late evening
  3. Use 6 knapsacks per acre for full coverage
- **Sources:** NPCK p.89, 96

## 2. Early Blight

**EB-001 — Favorable Conditions**
- **Triggers when:** Temperature ≥20°C AND humidity ≥80%
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Alternaria solani causes brown scorching spots on leaves. Warm humid conditions favour infection.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Spray Mancozeb from emergence, every 7 days
  2. Scout for brown concentric ring spots on older leaves
- **Sources:** NPCK p.89

---

## 3. Black Scurf

**BS-001 — Cold Wet Soil at Planting**
- **Triggers when:** Soil temperature ≤7°C OR (72h rain ≥30mm AND rain probability ≥60%)
- **Applies to:** Planting stage
- **Why it matters:** Rhizoctonia solani promoted by cold (<7°C) or waterlogged soil. Hard brown masses on tubers, dead tissue on stems.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Do NOT plant today — soil is too cold or too wet
  2. Wait for soil to warm above 7°C and drain
  3. Use certified disease-free seed
- **Sources:** NPCK p.29, TRAINING p.60

---

## 4. Bacterial Wilt

**BW-001 — Waterlogging Spread Risk**
- **Triggers when:** 72h cumulative rain ≥40mm
- **Applies to:** All stages
- **Why it matters:** Heavy rain carries Ralstonia solanacearum via surface runoff. Bacterial wilt is soil-persistent and devastating.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Check drainage channels are clear — runoff spreads bacterial wilt
  2. Inspect for wilting plants despite adequate moisture (diagnostic sign)
  3. If wilt found: rogue diseased plants immediately, do NOT compost them
  4. Rotate with non-solanaceous crops for >2 years if field infected
- **Sources:** NPCK p.27, 90, 122

---

## 5. Blackleg / Soft Rot

**BL-001 — Wet Field Risk**
- **Triggers when:** 48h rain ≥25mm AND humidity ≥85%
- **Applies to:** All stages
- **Why it matters:** Pectobacterium (blackleg/soft rot) thrives in wet, flooded fields. Slimy black stem bases, tuber rot.
- **Priority:** Medium
- **What to do:**
  1. Check drainage — do not allow standing water
  2. Do NOT hill up while soil is this wet — spreads disease
  3. If harvesting soon, wait for dry weather
- **Sources:** NPCK p.91, TRAINING p.63-64

## 6. Common Scab

**CS-001 — Dry Soil at Tuber Set**
- **Triggers when:** 7+ consecutive dry days during tuberization stage
- **Applies to:** Tuberization
- **Why it matters:** Streptomyces scab promoted by dry soil at tuber initiation. Rough, scabby skin reduces market value.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Irrigate NOW — soil moisture at tuber set is critical
  2. Maintain consistent moisture through tuber initiation
  3. Avoid over-liming (alkaline soil worsens scab)
- **Sources:** NPCK p.133, TRAINING p.64

## 7. Temperature Stress

**TEMP-001 — Heat Stress**
- **Triggers when:** Maximum temperature ≥21°C
- **Applies to:** Tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Above 21°C causes sharp decline in tuber formation. Above 29°C, little or no tubers form.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Irrigate to reduce soil temperature
  2. Apply mulch to conserve moisture and moderate temperature
  3. If consistently >25°C, expect reduced yield
- **Sources:** NPCK p.26

**TEMP-002 — Frost Alert**
- **Triggers when:** Minimum temperature ≤1°C
- **Applies to:** All stages
- **Why it matters:** Frost scorching destroys potato foliage. Can cause total crop loss. Primary climate hazard in Nyandarua County.
- **Priority:** Critical
- **What to do:**
  1. Apply mulch to protect crop tonight
  2. Assess damage — severely damaged crops may not recover
  3. Plan future planting to avoid frost-prone periods
- **Sources:** NPCK p.20, TRAINING p.163

## 8. Water Management

**WL-001 — Waterlogging Alert**
- **Triggers when:** 48h cumulative rain ≥40mm
- **Applies to:** All stages
- **Why it matters:** Waterlogging causes root suffocation, nutrient leaching, fungal diseases, and crop washout.
- **Priority:** Critical
- **What to do:**
  1. Clear all drainage channels and cut-off drains immediately
  2. Do NOT irrigate — soil is saturated
  3. Do NOT hill up — wet soil compaction spreads disease
  4. If planting: delay until soil drains
- **Sources:** NPCK p.20, 29, 54

**IRR-001 — Irrigation Needed**
- **Triggers when:** 7-day cumulative rain ≤25mm
- **Applies to:** Emergence, vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Potato needs minimum 25mm/week. Deficit reduces yield and quality.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Start irrigation — rainfall below threshold this week
  2. Use drip irrigation (recommended) — avoids blight risk
  3. Avoid sprinkler — promotes late blight
  4. Prioritise watering at tuber initiation and bulking stages
- **Sources:** NPCK p.26, 56

**DRY-001 — Drought Alert**
- **Triggers when:** 14+ consecutive dry days
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Prolonged dry spell causes crop failure and aphid/whitefly population surge.
- **Priority:** Critical
- **What to do:**
  1. Irrigate immediately — drip preferred
  2. Apply mulch
  3. Increase aphid scouting — dry conditions cause pest surge
- **Sources:** NPCK p.20

## 9. Pests

**APHID-001 — Aphid Surge in Dry Conditions**
- **Triggers when:** 7+ dry days AND temperature ≥18°C
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Drought causes aphid/whitefly surge. Aphids vector Potato Leaf Roll Virus and Potato Virus Y.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Check yellow water traps (6 per acre)
  2. Spray Alphacypermethrin or Dimethoate (30-40ml per 20L) if aphids detected
  3. Apply mineral oils to prevent virus transmission
  4. Rogue virus-symptomatic plants (leaf curling, mosaic)
- **Sources:** NPCK p.20, 91, 94, 105

**PTM-001 — Potato Tuber Moth — Warm Dry Risk**
- **Triggers when:** Temperature ≥20°C AND 5+ dry days
- **Applies to:** Bulking, maturation, harvest
- **Why it matters:** Warm and dry weather favours PTM. Larvae tunnel into tubers in field and storage.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Check ridges — re-earth up if any tubers exposed
  2. Maintain 25cm ridge height minimum
  3. Cut foliage at 80 days after planting
  4. Harvest immediately after dehaulming
- **Sources:** NPCK p.92, TRAINING p.68-70

**PTM-002 — Exposed Tubers After Heavy Rain/Wind**
- **Triggers when:** 24h rain ≥15mm AND wind gusts ≥20 km/h
- **Applies to:** Tuberization, bulking, maturation
- **Why it matters:** Rain and wind erode ridges, exposing tubers to PTM and greening.
- **Priority:** Medium
- **What to do:**
  1. Inspect ridges for exposed tubers
  2. Re-earth up immediately
- **Sources:** NPCK p.32, 92

**NEM-001 — Root-Knot Nematode**
- **Triggers when:** Soil temperature 20–30°C (optimal ~27°C)
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **Why it matters:** Meloidogyne root galling. New generation in 25 days under favourable conditions. No egg-laying below 14.2°C or above 31.7°C.
- **Priority:** Low
- **What to do:**
  1. Plant trap crops (Mexican marigold) between seasons if nematodes known
  2. Rotate with non-host crops
  3. Avoid furrow irrigation on sandy soils
- **Sources:** NPCK p.90, 124

**CW-001 — Cutworm at Emergence**
- **Triggers when:** 5+ dry days during emergence stage
- **Applies to:** Emergence, early vegetative
- **Why it matters:** Cutworms cut emerging shoots at ground level during dry spells.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Scout early morning for cut stems and foliage holes
  2. Spray Alphacypermethrin or Dimethoate (30-40ml per 20L) at 10 days after germination
  3. Hill up to cover stem bases
- **Sources:** NPCK p.93-94, TRAINING p.70-71

## 10. Field Operations and Harvest

**HILL-001 — Hilling in Wet Soil Warning**
- **Triggers when:** 24h rain ≥10mm during vegetative stage
- **Applies to:** Vegetative
- **Why it matters:** Hilling on wet soil causes compaction and spreads late blight and blackleg.
- **Priority:** Medium
- **What to do:**
  1. Do NOT hill today — soil is too wet
  2. Wait at least 2 days without rain
- **Sources:** NPCK p.34, TRAINING p.58

**HRV-001 — Harvest Window — Dry and Overcast**
- **Triggers when:** Rain probability ≤20% AND cloud cover ≥50%
- **Applies to:** Harvest stage
- **This is the ideal harvest window.**
- **Priority:** Opportunity
- **What to do:**
  1. Good day to harvest — dry soil with cloud cover
  2. Thumb test: skin should not slip
  3. Leave tubers on ground max 3 hours, then move to shade
  4. Do NOT expose to direct sunlight — causes greening
- **Sources:** NPCK p.67, TRAINING p.75

**HRV-002 — No Harvest — Wet Soil**
- **Triggers when:** Rain probability ≥50% during harvest stage
- **Applies to:** Harvest
- **Why it matters:** Wet harvest increases bruising, disease, and dirty tubers. Blackleg infection rises sharply.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Do NOT harvest — wait for dry conditions
  2. Wet-harvested tubers rot in storage
- **Sources:** NPCK p.67, TRAINING p.63

**HRV-003 — Sun Exposure Risk at Harvest**
- **Triggers when:** Cloud cover ≤30% AND max temperature ≥22°C
- **Applies to:** Harvest
- **Why it matters:** Clear skies cause rapid greening and sunscald.
- **Priority:** Medium
- **What to do:**
  1. Move tubers to shade within 3 hours
  2. Cover during transport
  3. Harvest early morning or late afternoon
- **Sources:** NPCK p.67, TRAINING p.75

## 11. Planting

**PLT-001 — Planting Window**
- **Triggers when:** 7-day rain ≥15mm AND soil temperature ≥8°C
- **This is the optimal planting window.**
- **Priority:** Opportunity
- **What to do:**
  1. Rains have started and soil is warm — plant now
  2. Use well-sprouted seed (3+ sprouts)
  3. 30cm spacing, 15cm depth
  4. Apply DAP at planting
- **Sources:** NPCK p.29-30, TRAINING p.129

**PLT-002 — Do Not Plant — Cold or Waterlogged**
- **Triggers when:** Soil temperature ≤7°C OR 48h rain ≥40mm
- **Why it matters:** Cold promotes black scurf. Waterlogging promotes blackleg and root rot.
- **Priority:** High
- **What to do:**
  1. Do NOT plant — soil too cold or too wet
  2. Wait for soil to warm and drain
- **Sources:** NPCK p.29

## 12. Fertilizer

**NLEACH-001 — Nitrogen Leaching Risk**
- **Triggers when:** 48h rain ≥30mm at 30–55 days after planting
- **Applies to:** Vegetative (40-50 DAP)
- **Why it matters:** Heavy rain at 40-50 DAP leaches nitrogen before crop demand peaks.
- **Priority:** Medium
- **What to do:**
  1. If N not applied yet, delay until rain eases
  2. If recently applied, consider supplemental top-dress at bulking
  3. Split N across the season
- **Sources:** NPCK p.101

**FRT-001 — Fertilizer Window**
- **Triggers when:** 1–10mm rain today AND rain probability next 6h ≤40%
- **Applies to:** Vegetative, tuberization, bulking
- **This is a fertilizer opportunity.**
- **Priority:** Opportunity
- **What to do:**
  1. Soil is moist but not waterlogged — good day to apply fertilizer
  2. Apply DAP at planting; CAN as top-dress
  3. Incorporate urea immediately after spreading
- **Sources:** NPCK p.101

## 13. Spray Windows

**SPR-001 — General Spray Window**
- **Triggers when:** Rain probability next 6h ≤20% AND wind <15 km/h
- **Priority:** Opportunity
- **What to do:**
  1. Good conditions for spraying — calm and dry
  2. Apply early morning or late evening
  3. Wear protective clothing
- **Sources:** NPCK p.85

**SPR-002 — No Spray — Rain or Wind**
- **Triggers when:** Rain probability next 6h ≥60% OR wind ≥15 km/h
- **Priority:** Informational
- **What to do:**
  1. Do NOT spray — chemicals will wash off or drift
  2. Wait for calm, dry conditions
- **Sources:** NPCK p.85, 91

## 14. Conflict Resolution

| Conflict | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Spray needed but rain/wind prevents it | **Weather wins.** Delay spray. But do NOT skip — apply at first available window. |
| Irrigation needed but soil is waterlogged | **Waterlogging wins.** Do not irrigate saturated soil. |
| Harvest season but soil is wet | **Wet harvest warning wins.** Delay harvest. |
| Hilling due but soil is wet | **Wet soil wins.** Delay hilling until soil dries (2+ days without rain). |

## 15. Key Thresholds Summary

| Parameter | Threshold | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Optimal growth temperature | 15–18°C daily average | Best vegetative growth |
| Tuberization decline | >21°C | Sharp yield reduction |
| Tuberization cessation | >29°C | No tubers form |
| Optimal soil temp for tubers | 15–24°C | Maximum initiation |
| Frost damage | ≤0°C | Foliage destroyed |
| Black scurf planting risk | Soil <7°C | Do not plant |
| Late blight trigger | ≥10°C for 2 days + RH ≥90% | Spray immediately |
| Blight acceleration | ≥15°C + RH ≥85% | Increase spray frequency |
| Weekly water need | 25mm/week minimum | Irrigation trigger |
| Seasonal rainfall | 400–800mm/season | Adequate for yield |
| Nematode optimal | ~27°C soil | Peak infestation |
| Nematode egg-laying range | 14.2–31.7°C | Below/above = no new generation |
| Curing temperature | 18–20°C | Wound healing |
| Curing humidity | 85–90% RH | 10–14 days |
| Storage temperature (ware) | 4–7°C | Long-term quality |
| Nitrogen leaching risk | Heavy rain at 40–50 DAP | Split applications |
| Potassium uptake peak | First 6 weeks | 70% of season demand |
| Dehaulming trigger | ≥50% haulms yellow, 100–120 DAE | 2 weeks before harvest |
