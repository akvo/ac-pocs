Persea Mite Host Range
Primary Hosts and Range
Persea mites primarily infest avocado trees (*Persea americana*) but have a wide host range including other fruit trees,
ornamentals, and weeds.
Fruit Tree Hosts
Affected fruit trees include carob, persimmon, various *Prunus* species (apricots, peaches, plums, nectarines), and certain
grape varieties.
Ornamental and Weed Hosts
Ornamentals like roses, acacia, and bamboo, along with weeds such as milkweed and sow thistle, also serve as hosts.
Additional Hosts
Other hosts include eucalyptus, malva spp., passionfruit, and castor bean.

Identifying Persea Mites
Detection Challenges
Due to their small size, Persea mites are difficult to detect directly;
presence is typically identified through the effects and damage resulting
from infestation, based on signs and symptoms.

Persea Mite Management Overview
Control Challenges
Persea mites are difficult to control due to their short life cycle, protective webbing, and wide host range, which allows them
to survive on alternative hosts.
Pesticide Use Concerns
The emergence of Persea mites may lead to increased pesticide use, contrasting with Kenya's minimal chemical use in
avocado production and challenges of market access especially in the EU due to pesticide residues .
Comprehensive Approach Needed
There is no single effective management strategy; a comprehensive control and management approach is required.

Chemical Control of Persea Mites
Chemicals in Kenya
Currently no chemicals are specifically registered for Persea mite control in Kenya, but severe infestations may warrant
chemical intervention to prevent damage and defoliation.
Economic Thresholds
Economic threshold levels must be determined before implementing control measures to guide effective timing.
Resistance and Selection
Frequent use of a limited number of pesticides with similar modes of action can lead to resistance development, emphasizing
the need for low-risk pesticides and spot treatments to conserve beneficial insects.

Role of Producers
Information Engagement
Engage with extension service providers and regulators for the latest information on approved pesticides and Persea mite
management.
Pesticide Use and Monitoring
Use registered pest control products and conduct regular surveillance and monitoring for Persea mites.
Practices and Reporting
Implement good agricultural practices for Persea mite management, report new pests/diseases, and source clean planting
material from certified producers.
Record Keeping and Safety
Maintain detailed records of agronomy, monitoring, interventions, and yields to assess the cost-benefit of control methods,
and ensure farm workers use proper PPE when applying pesticides.
